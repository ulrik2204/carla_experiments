English
The effect of different inputs and outputs used in the Openpilot system on autonomous driving model performance
Title: Analyzing the Impact of Different Inputs and Outputs in the Openpilot System on the Performance of Autonomous Driving Models

Commercial autonomous driving products, like Openpilot by comma.ai, are interesting from a research standpoint because they can be seen as a verification of the state-of-the-art and can introduce new problems and opportunities for research. At the same time these systems might be hard to research because of limited access to their system code and the different target audiences of research and commercial products. 

This thesis will explore and recreate parts of the Openpilot system to explore whether the Openpilot methods are suitable for research. As part of this thesis, a system may be developed to make some of the Openpilot methods more applicable for research purposes. The thesis will focus on recreating the dataset used to train Openpilot by using the CARLA Simulator. Using this dataset the thesis will explore the effects of the different inputs and outputs used in the Openpilot system and how these affect the performance of an autonomous driving model.
